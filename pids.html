Metadata and Required PIDs

Connecting the Research Ecosystem with Persistent Identifiers

Why Metadata Matters

Metadata is the "data about data," providing crucial context. When it's rich and standardized, it makes research datasets understandable not just to humans, but also to machines. This machine-actionability is fundamental for enabling search, discovery, and automation, thereby making the data truly Findable and Interoperable. PIDs are the most powerful tool for creating this machine-actionable web of connections.

The PIDs and Their Purpose

Persistent Identifiers are like a social security number for research entities. They ensure that projects, people, and institutions can be uniquely and reliably identified and linked, even if their names or locations change over time.
